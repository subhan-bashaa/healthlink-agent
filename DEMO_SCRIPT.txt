[1:00 — ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let's walk through how this system functions under the hood. 
First, the Intake node saves the user's issue into our state. 
Next, the Security Checkpoint inspects the input for prompt injections, screens for dangerous safety keywords like suicide, and scrubs personally identifiable information like phone numbers or SSNs.
If the check passes, the workflow routes to the Triage node, which runs our TriageOrchestrator. 
The orchestrator delegates work to either the FirstAidSpecialist sub-agent or the TeleHealthCoordinator sub-agent depending on the severity and nature of the issue.
Both sub-agents make calls to our custom Model Context Protocol server to retrieve verified clinic locations or first-aid steps.
Finally, the system pauses at a Human Approval gate, giving a medical coordinator the chance to review, approve, or deny the advice before it's sent to the patient via the Output node.
